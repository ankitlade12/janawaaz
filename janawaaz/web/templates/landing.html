A durable agent, not another portal

You tell it who you are once, in one plain sentence. It does the rest — every day, while you sleep.

1

It watches

Scheduled workflows sweep TRAI, SEBI and more, surviving flaky government sites with automatic retries. New papers are parsed and the comment deadline is extracted with a quoted evidence span.

2

It matches — then proves it

Your profile is matched semantically against every new consultation, and every candidate match must survive a deterministic verification gate before it can wake you up.

3

It speaks your language

Alerts arrive on Telegram in English, हिन्दी or मराठी via Sarvam AI — with the deadline, what changes for you, and exactly where to submit your comment.

Every alert shows its work

Embedding similarity alone fires garbage — “interested in agriculture” matches a telecom tariff paper because both mention “rural”. So every candidate match passes a gate, and every evaluated decision is written to a public, append-only audit ledger you can inspect.

Tier 1 — Confirmed

Similarity passes and a verifier answers a strict yes and quotes a span from the document that is string-checked against the actual text. Only this tier sends a push alert.

Tier 2 — Possible

Similarity passes but the evidence is weak or unverifiable. Recorded for review and never wakes you up.

Tier 3 — Rejected

The verifier says no. Evaluated candidates are recorded in the ledger so the decision is auditable.

If we can’t prove the match, we don’t wake you up. Nobody else in this space — at any price — shows their work.
