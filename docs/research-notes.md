# Research notes (2026-07-05)

Findings from a multi-source research sweep on the JanAwaaz premise. Extraction
completed from 14 fetched sources; adversarial verification pass was cut short,
so treat figures as single-source-checked. Key claims cross-checked against the
sources below before we changed any pitch copy.

## The 2015 net-neutrality story — corrected

Our old line ("nearly passed unnoticed") was wrong, and the truth is a better pitch:

- TRAI's OTT consultation (released 27 Mar 2015) was **118 pages with an obscure
  title**; the official comments table lists **27 institutional submissions**
  and no individual citizens ([trai.gov.in](https://www.trai.gov.in/consultation-paper-regulatory-framework-over-top-ott-services)).
- Over **1 million emails** did reach TRAI — but only after the AIB
  "Save The Internet" video (uploaded 11 Apr, 15 days into the window) and a
  volunteer team that condensed the paper into ~18 plain-language pages
  ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Net_neutrality_in_India),
  [Civil Society Online](https://www.civilsocietyonline.com/campaigns/inside-story-how-net-neutrality-was-won/)).
- **New framing:** mass participation was manufactured by volunteer
  intermediaries doing discovery + translation by hand. JanAwaaz automates that
  intermediary layer.

## USP check — quote-verified alerts remain unclaimed

- **Compliance.ai**: "Expert in the Loop" ML, enterprise compliance teams; no

- **FiscalNote PolicyNote** (Jun 2025): AI alerts + *Similar Bills* semantic
  matching — semantic matching is incumbent table stakes; no quote-grounding
  claims ([fiscalnote.com](https://fiscalnote.com/newsroom/fiscalnote-accelerates-ai-innovation-with-new-enhancements-to-policynote-for-legislative-forecasting-alerts-and-bill-discovery)).
- **Wolters Kluwer "Compliance Intelligence"** (announced Nov 2025): new
  entrant, human-SME-powered AI; no cited-evidence verification advertised
  ([wolterskluwer.com](https://www.wolterskluwer.com/en/expert-insights/introducing-compliance-intelligence-trusted-ai-for-when-you-have-to-be-right)).
- Consequence for the pitch: lead with **verification**, not with "automated
  semantic matching" (which incumbents already ship).

## Comment authenticity — why verification matters beyond UX

- NY AG investigation: **~18M of 22M** comments in the FCC's 2017 net-neutrality
  docket were fake; one student submitted 7.7M via automation; settlements
  ($3.7M Fluent et al.) established an **express-informed-consent** standard for
  submitting on someone's behalf ([ag.ny.gov](https://ag.ny.gov/sites/default/files/reports/oag-fakecommentsreport.pdf)).
- Product rule: comment-drafting help must be consent-first and human-submitted.
  Never automate submission.

## Adoption lessons (Mobile Vaani, IVR civic-tech)

- Missed-call IVR is a proven reach pattern for low-literacy, non-smartphone
  users; Mobile Vaani peaked at 100K monthly users across 25+ districts
  ([arXiv 2104.07901](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2104.07901)).
- Adoption required **offline volunteer mobilization**, retention required
  **visible outcomes**; funding ended the scale-up. Software alone doesn't
  reach this demographic — partners do (feeds the Civis-on-top thesis).

## WhatsApp economics (roadmap)
